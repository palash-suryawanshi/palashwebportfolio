Recent Work

SDSU Research Foundation

As a Research Assistant at SDSU, I have been actively involved in projects that leverage data science and machine learning to drive impactful insights. In one of my key projects, I extracted and analyzed 133,000+ financial articles from Seeking Alpha using Python (Scrapy), which improved data retrieval efficiency by 30%. This data was modeled for over 1,000 stock tickers (e.g., AMZN, AAPL) using Pandas, NumPy, and SQL, resulting in 15% faster query execution for financial analytics. Additionally, I integrated datasets from CRSP, Compustat, and I/B/E/S, enhancing financial data aggregation and improving stock trend prediction accuracy by 20%.

In another project, I applied NLP techniques and statistical transformations to refine unstructured data, improving data structuring efficiency and ensuring 99% data accuracy through techniques like outlier handling and Winsorization.

As a Graduate Assistant, I worked closely with faculty in developing and evaluating course materials for the Marketing Research program. Leveraging data analysis techniques, I conducted research on sustainability in online grocery shopping, where I synthesized insights from leading industry reports such as Deloitte, PwC, and McKinsey. This analysis identified three key trends that contributed to improving market efficiency. My contributions also enhanced student engagement by 40% through improved instructional design.

These projects reflect my commitment to combining data science methodologies with practical applications to deliver actionable insights that drive efficiency, improve decision-making, and contribute to meaningful outcomes in finance, marketing, and beyond.
